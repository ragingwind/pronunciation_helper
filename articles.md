# Should We Ban Zoos?
Zoos are very popular places for families to see wild animals. However, many people today think we should close all zoos.

On the good side, zoos protect endangered animals. They give them a safe place to live and good doctors. Also, children can learn to love nature by seeing real animals.

On the bad side, keeping wild animals in cages is cruel. Animals need space to run and play. In zoos, they often feel lonely and bored.

In my opinion, we should change how zoos work instead of closing them. Zoos should build larger spaces so animals can live happily. What do you think?

# Should We Stop Using Plastic Bags?

Plastic bags are everywhere in our lives. People use them at supermarkets, bakeries, and even at school. However, many countries now want to stop using them. Why is that?

First, plastic bags are very bad for the earth. They do not break down easily. A single bag can stay in the ground for hundreds of years. Sea animals often eat them by mistake and get sick.

On the other hand, plastic bags are cheap and light. They are also strong, so shoppers can carry heavy things without any trouble. Many small shops still need them.

In my opinion, we should slowly stop using plastic bags. We can bring cloth bags from home instead. If everyone tries a little, our planet will become much cleaner and safer for animals.

# Should Students Use Smartphones at School?

These days, almost every student has a smartphone. Some people say phones are helpful, but others think they cause many problems.

On the good side, smartphones help students find information quickly. If a student does not understand a word, he can search for it right away. Phones also keep students safe because they can call their parents after school.

However, smartphones can be very distracting. Many children play games or watch videos during class. They have lost their focus, and their grades have dropped.

In my opinion, schools should allow phones only during break time. That way, students can enjoy the good parts and avoid the bad ones.

# Should Students Wear School Uniforms?

Many schools around the world ask their students to wear uniforms. Some people love this idea, but others do not agree. First, uniforms help students feel equal. Rich or poor, everyone looks the same, so no one feels sad about clothes. Also, wearing a uniform saves time in the morning because students do not need to choose what to wear. However, some students say uniforms are boring. They want to show their own style and colors. In addition, uniforms can be uncomfortable in hot weather, especially in countries like Malaysia. In the end, both sides have strong points. Uniforms bring fairness and save time, but they can also limit freedom. What do you think? Should every school have a uniform, or should students choose their own clothes?

# The Amazing Journey of a Paper Airplane)

Have you ever made a paper airplane? It looks very simple, but it hides a big secret about science. When you fold a flat piece of paper, you create wings. These wings push the air down, and the air pushes the plane up. This invisible force is called "lift." A long time ago, a clever man named Leonardo da Vinci dreamed about flying machines. He drew many pictures of wings in his notebook. Hundreds of years later, the Wright Brothers built the first real airplane. They studied birds and paper models before their big flight. So, the next time you throw a paper plane across your room, remember something important. You are not just playing a game. You are following the footsteps of great inventors. Every small toy can teach us a huge lesson about the world.

# The Story of the Trojan Horse

Long ago in ancient Greece, there was a great war between the Greeks and the city of Troy. The war lasted for ten long years, and neither side could win. The strong walls of Troy were too high to climb, and the gates were too thick to break. The Greek soldiers were tired, hungry, and ready to give up. However, a clever man named Odysseus had a brilliant idea. He told his men to build a giant wooden horse and hide the best soldiers inside it. The Greeks left the horse in front of the city and pretended to sail away. The people of Troy thought the horse was a gift from the gods, so they happily pulled it into their city. That night, while everyone in Troy was sleeping, the hidden soldiers quietly came out of the horse. They opened the gates, and the Greek army rushed in. In the end, Troy was defeated because of one smart trick. This famous story teaches us that being clever is sometimes more powerful than being strong.

# The Boy Who Cried Wolf

There was once a young shepherd boy who lived in a small village near a green hill. Every day, he took his sheep to the field and watched them eat grass. The job was quiet, and the boy often felt bored and lonely. One afternoon, he decided to play a silly trick on the villagers. He ran down the hill and shouted, "Wolf! Wolf! A big wolf is chasing my sheep!" The kind villagers quickly grabbed sticks and ran up the hill to help him. But when they arrived, they saw no wolf at all. The boy laughed loudly at them. A few days later, he played the same trick again, and the villagers came running once more. They felt angry and tired. Then, one evening, a real wolf appeared behind the trees. The frightened boy screamed, "Wolf! Wolf! Please help me!" However, this time, nobody believed him, and nobody came. The wolf scared away all his sheep, and the boy learned a painful lesson. From that day, he understood that liars are not trusted, even when they finally tell the truth.
